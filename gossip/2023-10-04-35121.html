10 new measures to tackle knife crime announced by UK government

By Dott. Marisa Lamborghini
Published October 04, 2023

2019-07-15T13:48:00+01:00

BREAKING NEWS: UK government has announced new measures to tackle knife crime

The UK government has announced a number of new measures aimed at tackling knife crime in the country. The measures include:

A new public awareness campaign, led by the Met Police and funded by the government, which will target young people aged 15-24 with messages about the consequences of carrying knives and the importance of reporting any suspicious activity.

The creation of a new national database to track the sale of knives in England and Wales, which will help police identify dealers and take action against them.

A review of the law around knife crime, including looking at whether existing laws are effective and exploring the possibility of introducing new offences for carrying or using knives.

Increased funding for youth services and community groups to provide support for young people who may be vulnerable to knife crime.

The government has also announced that it will be working with the Met Police to increase the use of technology, such as body cameras and facial recognition software, to help identify suspects and bring them to justice.
