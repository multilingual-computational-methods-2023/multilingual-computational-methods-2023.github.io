Budget 2019-2020: A breakdown of the key points

The 2019-2020 Budget was presented to Parliament on Tuesday, February 18, 2019. Here is a breakdown of the key points:

1. The budget deficit for the year ending June 30, 2019, has been set at P64 billion. This represents an increase from last year's deficit of P57 billion.

2. The government will be investing more in infrastructure projects such as roads, bridges, and airports to stimulate economic growth. These investments are expected to create jobs and boost the economy.

3. Education is a key focus area for the government with an allocation of P140 billion going towards education programs and scholarships for students. This includes funding for teacher training and upgrading infrastructure in schools.

4. The government has allocated P25 billion for healthcare, which will go towards improving medical facilities, purchasing equipment, and providing more doctors and nurses to rural areas.

5. Environment and conservation are also receiving attention with an allocation of P10 billion going towards sustainable development projects such as renewable energy, green buildings, and reforestation efforts.

6. The government has allocated P3 billion for the implementation of the Philippine Development Plan 2017-2022, which aims to improve the lives of Filipinos through social development programs and economic growth.

7. The budget also includes funding for disaster response and mitigation measures, with an allocation of P5 billion going towards this effort.

8. Finally, the government has allocated P3 billion for the implementation of the Bangsamoro Basic Law, which aims to establish a self-governance agreement between the Philippines and the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF).

Overall, the 2019-2020 Budget is focused on investing in infrastructure, education, healthcare, environment, social development, disaster response, and the Bangsamoro Basic Law.
