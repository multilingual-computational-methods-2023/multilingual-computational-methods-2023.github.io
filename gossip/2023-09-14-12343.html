The Totally Legit Times

By Odette Pellet
Published September 14, 2023

2019-07-16T13:45:00+01:00

The first step in any project is to define the problem you are trying to solve. This is what we call the "problem statement." In this case, our problem statement is that we want to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the customer service experience at a retail store. We believe that by implementing certain strategies and technologies, we can achieve this goal.

Next, we need to identify the specific goals or objectives that we want to accomplish as a result of solving this problem. These could include things like increasing customer satisfaction, reducing wait times, improving response time to customer inquiries, optimizing store layout, etc.

Once we have our problem statement and goals in mind, we can start thinking about the technologies and strategies we might use to achieve them. This could include things like implementing a new customer service system, investing in interactive kiosks or self-service options, improving communication tools for employees, optimizing store layouts, etc.

Finally, we need to test our ideas and evaluate their effectiveness by measuring key performance indicators (KPIs) such as customer satisfaction scores, response times, wait times, sales revenue, and other metrics that are relevant to our goals. This will help us determine which strategies and technologies are most effective in achieving our desired outcomes.
