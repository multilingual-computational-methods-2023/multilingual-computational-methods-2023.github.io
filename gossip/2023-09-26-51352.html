2019-2020 budget

Finance Minister Arun Jaitley has presented the Union Budget for the financial year 2019-20 in Parliament. Here are some key points from the budget:

1. Personal income tax relief: The government will provide a standard deduction of Rs 5 lakh for salaried individuals and Rs 6,000 for non-salaried individuals for computing their taxable income. This will benefit over 80% of taxpayers.

2. Deduction for health insurance: Taxpayers who spend more than Rs 15,000 on health insurance premium will be allowed a deduction of the same amount from their total income while calculating their tax liability.

3. Higher education cess: A new education cess of 2% will be levied on taxable income above the threshold limit for education savings. This will help fund higher education in India.

4. Tax relief for electric vehicles: Taxpayers who buy electric vehicles will get a tax benefit of up to Rs 1.5 lakh, subject to certain conditions.

5. Standard deduction for professionals: Salaried professionals like doctors, engineers and lawyers will now be provided with a standard deduction of Rs 5 lakh instead of the existing nil deduction. This will help them save taxes and improve their financial position.

6. No tax on pension income: Pensioners will not have to pay any tax on their monthly pension income up to a certain limit.

7. Tax benefits for affordable housing: Homebuyers who purchase homes worth up to Rs 45 lakh will get a tax benefit of up to Rs 1.50 lakh, subject to certain conditions.

8. No tax on foreign travel expenses: Taxpayers who incur expenses up to Rs 20,000 for travel abroad will not have to pay any tax on those expenses.

9. Standard deduction for small businesses: Small scale industries and self-employed individuals will now be provided with a standard deduction of Rs 5 lakh instead of the existing nil deduction. This will help them save taxes and improve their financial position.

10. No tax on donations to NGOs: Donors who donate up to Rs 4,000 per year to eligible charitable trusts or institutions will not have to pay any tax.
