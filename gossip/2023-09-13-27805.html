2023 Global Temperature Rise

By Massimiliano Schär
Published September 13, 2023

2019-07-16T13:45:00+01:00

The new study, published in the journal Nature Communications, found that the average global temperature has already risen by about 1.8C since the pre-industrial era and is projected to continue rising at a rate of around 2.7C per century.

This increase in temperature has led to melting ice caps, rising sea levels, more frequent extreme weather events, and other impacts that are increasingly affecting people’s lives around the world.

The study also found that the Arctic is warming faster than the rest of the planet, with temperatures there increasing at a rate of about 2.5C per century since the pre-industrial era. This has led to widespread melting of sea ice and changes in ocean currents, which could have significant impacts on global climate patterns.

The study highlights the urgent need for action to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and limit the rise in temperature to below 1.5C above pre-industrial levels, as called for in the Paris Agreement.
