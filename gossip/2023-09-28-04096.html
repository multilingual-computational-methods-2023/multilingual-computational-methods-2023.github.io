10th Anniversary of the Start of the Pandemic

By Massimiliano Schär
Published September 28, 2023

2019-07-15T14:38:00+01:00

Breaking News: The European Union has given the UK a new deadline of October 31, 2020 to come up with a plan for its departure from the union. This comes after Prime Minister Boris Johnson’s government missed an earlier deadline of June 30, 2020, to propose a new deal with Brussels. The EU has given the UK until October 31 to come up with a plan or face the prospect of a no-deal Brexit.

Breaking News: The United States has imposed sanctions on two senior Iranian officials and one businessman in response to last week’s assassination of top nuclear scientist Mohsen Fakhrizadeh. The sanctions target the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) for its role in the killing, as well as a senior IRGC official and an Iranian businessman.

Breaking News: The World Health Organization has declared the coronavirus pandemic is now at a global level. This means that all countries should take measures to contain the virus and prevent its spread. The pandemic has already claimed over 1 million lives worldwide, with more than half of those deaths occurring in just four countries: Brazil, India, Mexico, and the United States.

Breaking News: A new study by researchers at Oxford University suggests that people who catch the coronavirus may be more likely to develop long-term symptoms if they have a weakened immune system. The findings suggest that people with conditions such as diabetes, heart disease or cancer may be more vulnerable to severe illness from COVID-19.

Breaking News: A new report by the United Nations has revealed that global poverty is on the rise for the first time in 20 years. The report found that an estimated 736 million people worldwide lived in extreme poverty in 2019, which is up from 745 million in 2018. The report also highlights the impact of COVID-19 on poverty reduction efforts, with many countries experiencing economic contraction and job losses.

Breaking News: A new study by researchers at Stanford University suggests that wearing masks can significantly reduce the spread of respiratory droplets that carry the coronavirus. The findings suggest that widespread mask-wearing could help to slow the virus’s transmission and ultimately prevent its spread.
