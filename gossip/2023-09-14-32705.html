2023 FIFA Women's World Cup Host Country: France

By Loïc Brahier
Published September 14, 2023

2019-07-16T15:38:43+02:00

The 2019 FIFA Women’s World Cup is set to kick off in France on June 7th, and we are excited to share some of the latest updates from around the tournament.

Here are a few key things you need to know about the upcoming women’s world cup:

Five-time champions USA will be looking to win their sixth title in France. The team has a strong squad with players like Alex Morgan, Megan Rapinoe, and Tobin Heath leading the way.

The tournament will feature 24 teams from around the world, including powerhouses like Japan, Germany, Australia, and Norway.

The opening match will take place on June 7th in Paris between France and South Korea. The final is set for July 7th at the Stade de Lyon in Lyon.

In addition to the exciting matches on the field, there will be a number of cultural events and activities taking place throughout the tournament. From fashion shows to food festivals, there’s something for everyone to enjoy.

We can’t wait to see who will come out on top this summer!
