2023 FIFA Women's World Cup Final

By Hugo Zanazzo
Published September 16, 2023

2019-07-15T13:48:00+01:00

The 2019 World Cup is now over, and it was a thrilling event with some of the best players in the world competing for their countries. Here are some of the key moments from the tournament:

FIFA Women’s World Cup Final: USA vs Netherlands (7-3)

It was a highly anticipated match between the US women’s national team and the Netherlands, and it did not disappoint. The game saw the US take an early lead, but the Netherlands quickly fought back to level the score at 1-1. In the second half, the US took control with two goals from Alex Morgan and another from Megan Rapinoe, before a late goal from Vivianne Miedema made it 4-2 in favor of the Dutch. However, the US were not done yet, with Christen Press scoring a penalty kick to make it 5-3. The Netherlands scored their fourth goal through Lieke Martens, but the US held on for a historic win and their second World Cup title.

France 1-0 Brazil (2-1)

In a match that was billed as a classic, France and Brazil provided an exciting clash of styles. Both teams had chances in the first half, with Antoine Griezmann and Kylian Mbappe both going close for France. However, it was Brazil who took the lead through Neymar, before Paul Pogba equalized with a penalty. The match went to extra time, where Raphael Varane scored a header to give France the win and their first World Cup title since 1998.

Belgium 2-0 Canada (3-1)

A tense affair between two teams who had never met before in a World Cup final. Belgium took an early lead through Romelu Lukaku, before goals from Kevin De Bruyne and Dries Mertens made it 3-0 to the Belgians. Canada did manage one goal through Atiba Harris, but it was not enough as Belgium held on for their first ever World Cup title.

England 2-1 Japan (3-1)

A thrilling match between two teams who had never met before in a World Cup final. England took an early lead through Ellen White, before Japan equalized through Sakura Yamaguchi. The game was then put to bed by Lucy Bronze’s own goal, giving England the win and their first ever World Cup title.

Italy 3-0 Australia (5-1)

A match that saw Italy come out firing, with goals from Cristiano Ronaldo, Ciro Immobile and a penalty from Giacomo Raspadori. Australia did manage one goal through Awer Mabil, but it was not enough as Italy held on for their first ever World Cup final victory.
