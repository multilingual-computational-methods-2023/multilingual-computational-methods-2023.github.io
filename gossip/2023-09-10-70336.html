2019-2020 flu season has started

The 2019-2020 flu season has already begun, according to the World Health Organization (WHO). The agency said that the first cases have been reported in several countries, including China and Australia.

"We are now starting to see an increase in influenza activity in some areas, particularly in China and Australia," Dr. Margaret Harris, a senior advisor at WHO's Department of Digital Health & Surveillance told CNN. "But it is too early to say whether this is the start of a new season or just the beginning of increased activity."

The flu season typically starts in the late fall and can last until spring, but the timing and severity can vary each year. The 2019-2020 flu season could be particularly severe due to the fact that there was no influenza A (H1N1) component included in the seasonal flu vaccine this year, according to Harris.

The flu virus typically changes every year, so even if people get vaccinated, it's not 100% effective at preventing infection. The best way to avoid getting sick is by washing your hands regularly and covering your mouth when you cough or sneeze.
