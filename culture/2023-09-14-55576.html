5 Surprising Things That Happen When You Stop Using Social Media

By Livio Savorgnan
Published September 14, 2023

2019-07-16T13:45:00+01:00

The following is a breaking news story. It was published on July 16, 2019. Please click here to read the full article.

A new study has found that the use of social media can have negative effects on mental health and well-being. The study, which involved over 500 young adults, found that individuals who spent more time on social media had a higher risk of experiencing symptoms of anxiety and depression. Additionally, the study revealed that the longer someone spends on social media, the greater their likelihood of engaging in negative behaviors such as cyberbullying and spreading rumors online.

The findings suggest that social media use should be approached with caution and may warrant further research into potential interventions to mitigate its negative effects on mental health.
