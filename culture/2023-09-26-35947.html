Breaking News: The European Union has agreed to provide a grant of €8 million to support the implementation of the National Education Strategy in Georgia. This decision was made during the meeting of the EU’s foreign ministers, which took place on July 16-21, 2019, in Luxembourg.

The grant will be used to implement the National Education Strategy, which aims to improve access to quality education and increase educational opportunities for all students in Georgia. The strategy includes measures such as increasing the number of school places, improving teacher qualifications, and developing digital literacy skills.

This decision is a significant step towards supporting Georgia’s efforts to improve its education system and promote sustainable development. It demonstrates the EU’s commitment to working with Georgia on issues of mutual interest, including education, democracy, and human rights.
