2019-2020 budget proposal includes funding for new mental health center

The 2019-2020 state budget proposal, which was released on Friday, includes $5 million in funding for a new mental health center in the city of Bridgeport. The proposed budget also includes $3.6 million for mental health services in Fairfield County and $1.4 million for substance abuse treatment services.

The mental health center will be located in Bridgeport and is expected to provide comprehensive mental health care services to residents, including crisis stabilization, outpatient therapy, medication management, and support groups. The center is designed to help individuals who are struggling with mental illness or addiction and need immediate assistance.

In addition to the mental health center funding, the budget proposal includes $10 million for affordable housing initiatives, which will help provide safe and accessible housing options for low-income families in Connecticut. This investment is expected to support economic growth and improve the overall quality of life for residents across the state.

The proposed budget also includes $25 million for education funding, including $14 million for public schools and $9 million for higher education institutions. These investments are expected to help ensure that students have access to high-quality educational opportunities and prepare them for success in the future.

Overall, this 2019-2020 budget proposal is a strong commitment to investing in the health and well-being of Connecticut residents. With these important initiatives, the state is taking steps towards creating a more equitable and prosperous society for everyone.
