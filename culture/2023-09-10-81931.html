2023 Global Climate Action Summit Location: San Francisco, California, United States

By Guillaume Cattin
Published September 10, 2023

2019-07-16T15:34:00+08:00

The 2019 Global Climate Action Summit is taking place in San Francisco from September 11 to 13, 2019. The summit will bring together leaders from around the world to discuss and take action on climate change.

The summit aims to accelerate progress towards a sustainable future by highlighting solutions that can reduce greenhouse gas emissions, increase resilience to climate impacts, and promote clean energy and economic growth.

Here are some of the key themes and speakers expected at the 2019 Global Climate Action Summit:

1. Climate leadership: Leaders from around the world will discuss their commitments to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and building a sustainable future.

2. Resilience in action: Experts will share case studies on how communities are adapting to climate impacts, such as wildfires, floods, and droughts.

3. Clean energy solutions: Leaders from around the world will discuss progress towards transitioning to clean energy sources, including solar, wind, and hydropower.

4. Economic growth: Experts will explore ways to promote sustainable economic development and create jobs in the process.

5. Partnerships for action: Representatives from international organizations, businesses, and civil society groups will discuss how they can work together to address climate change.

6. Youth leadership: Young people will share their perspectives on the urgent need for action on climate change and the role they can play in driving change.

7. Actionable solutions: Leaders and experts will share practical solutions that individuals and businesses can take to reduce their carbon footprint and support sustainable development.

8. Finance: Representatives from financial institutions, governments, and civil society organizations will discuss how to mobilize more funding for climate action.

9. Science-based targets: Scientists will present the latest research on what it takes to limit global warming to below 2 degrees Celsius, including the role of land use changes and other measures.

10. Innovation: Leaders from around the world will discuss new technologies and solutions that can help accelerate progress towards a sustainable future.
