1000-year-old olive tree in Israel has produced a fruit for the first time

The world's oldest known olive tree, which is located in the Jezreel Valley of Israel, has produced a fruit for the first time. The tree is believed to be over 1,000 years old and was recently measured at around 75 feet tall with a trunk diameter of about 3 feet. The olive tree is estimated to have been growing since the year 429 AD, making it one of the oldest known living organisms on Earth.
