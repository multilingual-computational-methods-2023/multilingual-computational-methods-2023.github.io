A new study has found that a type of artificial intelligence (AI) technology can predict the likelihood of a patient developing Alzheimer's disease up to four years before symptoms appear. The researchers used machine learning algorithms to analyze brain scans and identify patterns associated with cognitive decline in people who had not yet developed dementia. The results suggest that early detection could lead to earlier interventions and potentially even prevent the condition from progressing.
