10.3 million people in the US have received a COVID-19 vaccine, according to CDC data

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) said that as of Friday morning, approximately 10.3 million people in the United States have received at least one dose of the Pfizer-BioNTech or Moderna coronavirus vaccine. This includes more than 5.2 million people who have received both doses. The CDC data also shows that over 47 million doses of the vaccine have been distributed to states and healthcare providers for administration, but not all of those doses have yet been administered.
