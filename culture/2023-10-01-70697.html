Breaking News: July 16, 2019

Here are the top stories making headlines around the world today.

1. UK PM Theresa May steps down as Conservative leader after Brexit failure

British Prime Minister Theresa May has announced she will step down as leader of the ruling Conservatives on June 7th, after failing to secure a deal for Britain's departure from the European Union.

2. Iran says it has seized foreign oil tanker in Gulf

Iranian forces have seized a British-flagged oil tanker in the Strait of Hormuz, according to the country's Revolutionary Guard. The move comes after US President Donald Trump threatened to impose new sanctions on Tehran.

3. Hong Kong protests continue as police use tear gas and rubber bullets

Hong Kong protesters are continuing their demonstrations against a controversial extradition bill, with police using tear gas and rubber bullets in an attempt to disperse crowds.

4. Japan's Princess Mako visits Africa to promote conservation efforts

Japanese Princess Mako has arrived in Kenya to launch a campaign to protect elephants and other endangered species in the country. The visit comes as part of her effort to promote conservation efforts around the world.

5. Breaking down barriers: How a new app is helping deaf people communicate

A new app called SignaLink is being hailed as a game-changer for deaf people who want to communicate with others using sign language. It allows users to translate signed messages into text, and vice versa, in real time.
