BREAKING NEWS: The government has announced a new policy to provide free meals for students in schools. The policy will be implemented from the next academic year, starting from September 2020. This decision was taken after the government reviewed the current situation and realized that many students were facing financial difficulties due to the pandemic.

The policy will cover all students studying in secondary schools, colleges, and universities across the country. The meals will be provided by the government through a central kitchen system where cooked food will be prepared and delivered to schools. The government has also announced that it will provide nutritious food options such as fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

The policy aims to ensure that students have access to quality education while also providing them with nutritional support. This decision is expected to benefit millions of students across the country who are currently facing financial constraints due to the pandemic. The government has also announced that it will provide additional resources to schools to ensure that they can implement this policy effectively.

The announcement comes after months of advocacy by various stakeholders, including educationists, parents, and students themselves. They had been demanding free meals for students in schools as a means of ensuring equal access to education despite financial constraints. The government's decision is expected to have a positive impact on the lives of millions of students across the country.
