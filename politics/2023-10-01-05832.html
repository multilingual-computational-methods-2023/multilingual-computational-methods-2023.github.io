201-2020 Budget Speech

By Gino Hofmann
Published October 01, 2023

2019-2020 budget

Cape Town - The National Treasury has released the 2019/2020 Budget Speech, which outlines government's priorities and key initiatives for the year ahead. Here are some of the highlights:

The budget speech was delivered by Finance Minister Tito Mboweni in Parliament on Wednesday.

Here are some of the key points from the speech:

- The budget aims to reduce poverty and inequality by investing in education, health care, and social protection.

- The government will increase funding for education by R4 billion, which includes a 10% increase in funding for basic education and an additional R500 million for higher education.

- Health care will receive R36 billion, with an emphasis on improving the quality of health services and reducing wait times.

- Social protection will be increased by R8 billion, including an extra R2 billion for the old age pension and a new grant for young people aged 18 to 24.

- The budget also includes measures to address corruption, such as increasing funding for the National Prosecuting Authority by R500 million and investing in the South African Revenue Service (SARS) to improve its capacity to collect taxes.

- The government will also invest in infrastructure development, with an additional R29 billion going towards building roads, bridges, and other critical infrastructure projects.

- Finally, the budget includes measures to promote economic growth, such as investing R1.6 billion in small business support initiatives and creating a new agency to promote tourism.
