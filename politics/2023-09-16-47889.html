8 million euro grant agreement signed for renewable energy development in the Western Balkans Source: European Union Delegation to Montenegro

By Aude Felber Burri
Published September 16, 2023

2019-06-30T17:54:00+01:00

Breaking News: The European Union has agreed to provide a grant of €8 million to support the development of renewable energy in the Western Balkans. This is part of the EU’s overall commitment to promoting sustainable energy and climate action across the region. The grant will be used to finance projects that promote the use of renewable energy sources, such as solar, wind, hydroelectricity, and geothermal power, among others. The project will also support capacity building activities for local stakeholders in the development and implementation of renewable energy projects. This is a significant step towards achieving the EU’s goals of reducing greenhouse gas emissions and promoting sustainable energy in the Western Balkans.
