10.3 million people in the US have received a COVID-19 vaccine, according to the CDC

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) said Tuesday that more than 10.3 million people in the United States have received at least one dose of the Pfizer-BioNTech or Moderna coronavirus vaccine as of Monday. The agency estimates that over 87 million doses of the vaccines have been distributed to states and healthcare providers for administration. As of Tuesday, nearly 29 million individuals have received both doses of the vaccine.
