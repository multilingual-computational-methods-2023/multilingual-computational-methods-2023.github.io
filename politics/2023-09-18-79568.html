10.5 million people in the US have received a COVID-19 vaccine, according to CDC data

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) reported that as of Friday, Jan. 8, 2021, approximately 10.5 million people in the United States have received at least one dose of the COVID-19 vaccine. This number includes both healthcare workers and individuals aged 65 years or older, who are currently eligible to receive the vaccine under current guidelines. The CDC estimates that over 30 million doses of the Pfizer-BioNTech and Moderna vaccines have been distributed to states and healthcare providers as of Jan. 8.
