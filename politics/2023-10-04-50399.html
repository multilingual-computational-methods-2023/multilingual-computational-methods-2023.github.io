Breaking News: The United States and China have reached a trade deal that will see the two countries reduce their trade deficit by $200 billion over the next two years. This is according to reports from multiple sources, including CNBC and Reuters. The deal comes after months of negotiations between the US and China aimed at resolving their ongoing trade war. As part of the agreement, China has agreed to purchase an additional $36 billion worth of goods and services from the United States over the next two years, while the US will reduce its trade deficit by $120 billion. The deal also includes commitments to intellectual property rights and technology transfer. While details have not been released yet, sources suggest that the agreement is expected to be signed in the coming days.
