2019-2020 budget proposal includes $5 million for new police station in Westport

By John Nickerson

| john.nickerson@masslive.com | MassLive

The 2019-2020 state budget proposal released by Gov. Charlie Baker on Tuesday includes $5 million to help fund a new police station in Westport, according to the governor's office.

The proposed budget also includes $47 million for public safety initiatives, including funding for local law enforcement agencies and community violence prevention programs.

"This budget continues our commitment to keeping communities safe by investing in critical public safety initiatives," said Gov. Baker in a statement. "We are making historic investments in police stations and community-based violence prevention programs, which will help reduce crime rates and improve the quality of life for all residents."

The proposed budget also includes $25 million for the state's Department of Children and Families to support child welfare services, as well as funding for mental health services and substance abuse treatment.

In addition, the proposal includes $10 million in grants for community colleges to expand access to job training programs, and $3 million for workforce development initiatives.

The budget also proposes a 2.5% increase in the state's minimum wage to $14 an hour, effective Dec. 31, 2019.
