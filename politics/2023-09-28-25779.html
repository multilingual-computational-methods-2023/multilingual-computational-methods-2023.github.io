BREAKING NEWS: The first case of COVID-19 has been confirmed in the United States, according to a statement from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). The patient is a person who recently traveled to Wuhan, China. The CDC says it is monitoring the situation closely and working with other health agencies to investigate the virus's origins and spread.

The news comes as the World Health Organization (WHO) declared the outbreak of COVID-19 a global pandemic on Wednesday, as the number of cases continues to rise in many countries around the world. The WHO says it is taking steps to ensure that countries have the resources they need to respond to the virus and prevent its spread.

The CDC has issued guidelines for travelers returning from China, including providing information about COVID-19 symptoms and recommendations on how to reduce the risk of exposure to the virus.
