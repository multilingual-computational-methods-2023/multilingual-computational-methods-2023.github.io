The Totally Legit Times

2019 Summer Olympics

By Ghislaine Mettler Ritter
Published September 27, 2023

2019-07-15T14:36:00+08:00

The 2019 Summer Olympics in Tokyo, Japan are now history. The Games were held from July 24 to August 9, 2019. Here are some of the highlights of the event:

- Usain Bolt (Jamaica) won his fourth Olympic gold medal in the men's 100m dash, setting a new world record time of 9.58 seconds.

- Allyson Felix (USA) won her fifth Olympic gold medal in the women's 4x400m relay, with teammates English Gardner, Michael Cherry, and Javianne Oliver.

- Eileen Gu (China) won a surprise victory in the women's short track speed skating event, becoming the first Asian athlete to win an individual Olympic gold medal in that sport.

- Yusra Mardini of Syria won a bronze medal in the women's 100m freestyle swimming race, setting a new world record time of 53.28 seconds.

- Greg Rutherford (Great Britain) won a surprise victory in the men's long jump event, leaping 8.46 meters to win by just one centimeter over his nearest rival.

- Katie Ledecky (USA) won five gold medals in total, including four in swimming and one in freestyle relay. She also set new world records in the women's 1500m freestyle and 4x200m freestyle relays.

- The USA men's basketball team won their first Olympic gold medal since 1996, defeating Spain 107-100 in a thrilling final game.

Overall, the 2019 Summer Olympics were a success, with many memorable moments and achievements by athletes from around the world.
