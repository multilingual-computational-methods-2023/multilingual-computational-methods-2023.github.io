BREAKING NEWS: New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio has announced a new plan to combat the city's rising homelessness crisis. The plan includes investing $8 billion in affordable housing, job training and mental health services for those experiencing homelessness.

The plan will also create 9,000 new shelter beds and expand outreach programs to connect people with resources they need to get off the streets.

"We have a moral obligation to ensure that everyone has a safe and stable place to call home," de Blasio said in a statement. "This comprehensive approach will help us take significant steps towards ending homelessness in our city."

The plan comes as the number of people experiencing homelessness in New York City has increased by 40% since 2013, with many individuals and families struggling to find stable housing due to rising rents, evictions, and a lack of affordable options.
