100 Light Years from Home: A New Exoplanet with Water Vapor

By Sig.ra Fernanda Goldstein
Published September 27, 2023

2019-07-15T14:38:00+01:00

Astronomers have discovered a new exoplanet that is located about 100 light years away from Earth, and it has an atmosphere that contains water vapor. This discovery could potentially lead to the development of more accurate ways to measure the properties of other planets in our galaxy.

The planet, named TOI-700 d, was discovered by a high school student intern at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center. The discovery was announced today by NASA and the University of California, Santa Cruz.

"This is an exciting time for astronomy," said Sarah Seiffert, a research scientist at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center who worked on the discovery. "We are now able to study planets outside our solar system in unprecedented detail, and this newfound knowledge will help us better understand how planets form and evolve."

TOI-700 d is an Earth-sized planet that orbits a star called TOI-700, which is located about 100 light years away from Earth. "It demonstrates that even high school students can make important contributions to our understanding of the universe."
