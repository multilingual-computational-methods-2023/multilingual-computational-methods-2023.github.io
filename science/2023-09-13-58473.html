10.2 million people in the US have now received at least one dose of a COVID-19 vaccine

The number of people who have received at least one dose of a coronavirus vaccine has reached 10.2 million, according to data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. This represents about 3% of the U.S. population. The CDC said that more than 57 million doses of the vaccines have been distributed to states and healthcare providers so far. As of Monday, more than 41.6 million people had received at least one dose, while 29.8 million were fully vaccinated, meaning they had received two doses.
