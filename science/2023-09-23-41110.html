Breaking news: Buhari signs bill to establish National Commission for old people in Nigeria

President Muhammadu Buhari has signed the bill into law establishing a national commission for old people in Nigeria. The bill was passed by both chambers of the National Assembly last week. According to a statement from his media office, Buhari signed the bill on Monday at the Presidential Villa, Abuja. The establishment of the National Commission for Old People is aimed at ensuring that older persons are given the necessary support and care they require to maintain their health and well-being. The commission will be responsible for promoting the welfare of old people in Nigeria, coordinating with relevant stakeholders to provide them with quality healthcare services, and advocating for their rights and interests. The bill was sponsored by Senate President Bukola Saraki and House of Representatives Speaker Yakubu Dogara.
