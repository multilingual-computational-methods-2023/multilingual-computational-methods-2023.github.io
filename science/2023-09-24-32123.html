The Totally Legit Times

By Amina Kunz
Published September 24, 2023

1960s civil rights activist Stokely Carmichael, who helped launch the Black Power movement, has died at age 78.

Carmichael was a powerful voice in the struggle for African American equality and civil rights during the 1950s and 1960s. He was known for his fiery speeches and his advocacy of black self-defense and separatism.

In 2015, Carmichael was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama, who praised him as a "true American patriot" and "a pioneer in the civil rights movement."
