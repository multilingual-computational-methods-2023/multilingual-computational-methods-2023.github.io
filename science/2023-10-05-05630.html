100,000-strong march in London against Trump's visit

By Press Association Share

By Associated Press Reporter

Around 100,000 people have taken to the streets of central London to protest against US President Donald Trump's upcoming state visit.

The demonstration was estimated by police and organisers to be one of the largest in recent years, with a diverse crowd chanting "No Trump, no KKK, no fascist UK" as they walked through the city.

Protesters held banners reading "Dump Trump" and "Impeach Trump", while some carried signs calling for a boycott of the Trump International Hotel in London.

The protest was sparked by Mr Trump's decision to cancel a planned visit to Manchester, where he had been due to hold a rally on June 1st.

The move followed protests against him during his previous visits to the UK, including a mass demonstration outside Buckingham Palace that drew hundreds of thousands of people.
