10.5 million people in the US have received their first dose of COVID-19 vaccine

By CNN's Jason Kessler and Deanna Hackney

Updated 8:24 AM ET, Thu January 7, 2021

(CNN) The US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) reported Thursday that more than 10.5 million people have received their first dose of the COVID-19 vaccine in the United States as of Wednesday afternoon.

The agency said that over 367,000 doses were administered on Tuesday alone, bringing the total number of doses distributed to states and healthcare providers to about 24.8 million.

According to the CDC, about 5.9 million people have received both doses needed for full vaccination against COVID-19.

The US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) authorized the use of the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine for emergency use in December, followed by Moderna's authorization on Friday. Both vaccines require two doses to be fully effective.
