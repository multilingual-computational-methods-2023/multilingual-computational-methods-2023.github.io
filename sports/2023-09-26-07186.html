The first-ever World Cup to be held in Africa will take place in Egypt from June 14 to July 13, 2018. The tournament will feature 32 teams playing for the title of world champion. The matches will be played at various venues across Cairo, Giza and other cities in Egypt.

The World Cup is an international football competition that takes place every four years. It is organized by FIFA, the governing body for football worldwide. The 2018 tournament marks the first time that Africa has hosted the event since 2006 when South Africa hosted it.

Egypt has a rich history in football and is home to many famous players and coaches who have contributed significantly to the sport around the world. The country has also hosted several major international tournaments, including the African Cup of Nations, which is held every four years and features some of the best African teams competing for the title.
