The first official image of the new Samsung Galaxy S20 has been leaked online, and it looks stunning.

Samsung has just released its latest flagship smartphone, the Galaxy S20, and while we already have some hands-on experience with the device, leaks continue to pop up online giving us a glimpse at what the phone could look like when it's fully unboxed. The first official image of the Samsung Galaxy S20 has just been leaked online, and it looks stunning.

The image shows off the phone's sleek design, with a camera module on the back that appears to have three lenses and a flash. There's also a hole-punch cutout in the screen for the front-facing camera, which is expected to be an 8-megapixel selfie camera.

The Samsung Galaxy S20 is rumored to come with a 6.2-inch Dynamic AMOLED display that could have a resolution of 1440 x 3200 pixels and support for HDR10+. The phone is also expected to feature a Snapdragon 865 processor, 12GB of RAM, and up to 16GB of internal storage.

The camera module on the back of the Samsung Galaxy S20 is rumored to have a 48-megapixel primary sensor, which could be a combination of a 12-megapixel wide-angle lens and a 3D depth sensor. There's also expected to be an ultra-wide camera, a telephoto camera, and a flash.

The Samsung Galaxy S20 is also rumored to have a 4,000mAh battery that supports 15W fast charging, 9W wireless charging, and 3W reverse wireless charging. The phone is also expected to come with Bluetooth 5.0 support, GPS, and an in-display fingerprint scanner.

The Samsung Galaxy S20 is rumored to be unveiled on February 6th at a virtual event, so we'll have to wait until then to see the final product.
