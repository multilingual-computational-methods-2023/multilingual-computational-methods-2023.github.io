50% of plastic in the ocean comes from single-use products

By Roman Petruzzi-Greggio
Published September 16, 2023

2019-06-30T17:45:00+01:00

A new study has found that a type of plastic commonly used in single-use products such as straws, bags and bottles is likely to enter the ocean at an alarming rate. The study suggests that by 2050, there could be more plastic in the ocean than fish. This is a major concern for marine conservationists who are working to protect our oceans and the wildlife that lives within them.

The study was carried out by the Ocean Conservancy and the Association of Public Art and was published in the journal Marine Pollution Bulletin. It found that the most common type of plastic pollution in the ocean is polyethylene terephthalate (PET), which is used to make single-use products such as straws, bags and bottles.

The study suggests that by 2050, there could be more than 15 billion tons of plastic in the ocean, which is more than all the fish in the world today. This is a major concern for marine conservationists who are working to protect our oceans and the wildlife that lives within them.

Marine biologists have been warning about the impact of plastic pollution on marine life for years. They say that plastics can harm or kill animals that ingest it, as well as clog up their digestive systems and cause health problems. In addition, plastics break down into microplastics, which can be eaten by small fish and eventually make their way up the food chain to humans.

The study highlights the need for action to reduce plastic pollution in our oceans. This includes reducing the use of single-use products such as straws and bottles, recycling plastic waste, and supporting marine conservation efforts. It is important that we all take responsibility for reducing plastic pollution and protecting our oceans for future generations.
