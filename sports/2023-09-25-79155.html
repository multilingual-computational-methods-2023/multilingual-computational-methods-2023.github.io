10.3 million people in the US have received a COVID-19 vaccine, according to CDC data

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) reported that as of January 6, 2021, approximately 10.3 million people in the United States have received at least one dose of the Pfizer-BioNTech or Moderna COVID-19 vaccine. This represents about 45% of the total U.S. population ages 18 and older.

The CDC also reported that over 26 million doses of the vaccines have been distributed to states, health care providers, and other entities for administration as of January 6, 2021.
