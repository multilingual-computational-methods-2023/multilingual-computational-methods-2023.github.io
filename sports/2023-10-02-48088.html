20th Annual Critics' Choice Television Awards

By Massimiliano Schär
Published October 02, 2023

2019-07-16T13:45:00+01:00

The 2018/19 season was a memorable one for the National Theatre. The theatre company produced some of its most successful and critically acclaimed productions in recent years, including Angels in America, The Ferryman, and Network. In this article, we take a look back at some of the highlights from the past year.

Angels in America: Part One and Two

The first part of Tony Kushner’s epic drama was directed by Marianne Elliott and opened in April 2018. It starred Andrew Garfield and Nathan Lane, among others. The second part, which focused on the aftermath of the AIDS crisis, opened in July 2018 and also featured a star-studded cast including Denzel Washington and Frances McDormand.

The Ferryman

This Jez Butterworth drama, directed by Sam Mendes, opened in October 2018 and was set on a farm in County Armagh during the Troubles in Northern Ireland. It starred Gary Lewis, Judi Dench and Ian-McKellen, among others. The play won four Tony Awards including Best Play.

Directed by Ivo van Hove, Network opened in April 2018 and was a dark comedy about television news anchorman Howard Beale’s descent into madness. It starred Bryan Cranston and Tatiana Maslany, among others.

Breaking the Code

This stage adaptation of the classic thriller film The Third Man, directed by Stephen Daldry, opened in March 2018. It starred Sir Ian McKellen and Richard Armitage, among others.

Directed by Marianne Elliott, this musical based on the hit movie opened in February 2019. It starred Idris Elba and Taylor Swift, among others.
