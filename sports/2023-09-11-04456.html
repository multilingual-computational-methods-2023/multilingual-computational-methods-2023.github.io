2019-2020 Academic Year in Review

By Anne-Laure Ziegler
Published September 11, 2023

2019-07-16T13:45:00+01:00

The 2018-2019 academic year is coming to an end, and it has been a wild ride for everyone involved. From new faces joining the community to new initiatives being launched, there have been so many exciting moments that we'll never forget. Here are some of our favorite memories from this past year:

1. The launch of the new Writing Center: This fall, the Writing Center celebrated its grand opening with a ribbon-cutting ceremony and open house for students. With dedicated staff members and resources to help students improve their writing skills, we're excited to see how this will benefit our community in the coming years.

2. The return of the Coffee House: Our beloved Coffee House made its triumphant return this year with a new location in the Student Union. Students can now enjoy free coffee and baked goods while enjoying live music, poetry readings, and other events throughout the semester.

3. The opening of the new Science Building: After years of construction, the new Science Building opened its doors to students this fall. With state-of-the-art facilities and resources for biology, chemistry, physics, and more, we're excited to see how this will benefit our science students in the coming years.

4. The launch of the new Career Center: This spring, the Career Center celebrated its grand opening with a ribbon-cutting ceremony and open house for students. With new resources like career counseling, job shadowing opportunities, and a new resume workshop, we're excited to see how this will benefit our community in the coming years.

5. The return of the Student Government Association: After a year off due to budget cuts, the Student Government Association (SGA) returned this fall with a new slate of officers and initiatives for students to get involved with. From organizing events like the Welcome Back Picnic to advocating for student concerns on campus, we're excited to see how SGA will continue to shape our community in the coming years.

6. The launch of the new Public Safety Office: This fall, the Public Safety Office opened its doors to students and staff with a focus on safety and security on campus. With new measures like an increased police presence and a new emergency response plan, we're excited to see how this will benefit our community in the coming years.

7. The opening of the new Recreation Center: This fall, the Recreation Center celebrated its grand opening with a ribbon-cutting ceremony and open house for students. With new equipment, amenities, and programs like yoga and spin classes, we're excited to see how this will benefit our community in the coming years.

8. The launch of the new Multicultural Center: This fall, the Multicultural Center celebrated its grand opening with a ribbon-cutting ceremony and open house for students. With resources and initiatives focused on supporting underrepresented communities on campus, we're excited to see how this will benefit our community in the coming years.

9. The return of the Student Organizations Fair: This spring, the Student Organizations Fair returned to campus with new organizations and opportunities for students to get involved with. From sports teams to clubs focused on social justice issues, we're excited to see how this will continue to shape our community in the coming years.

10. The launch of the new Career Development Center: This fall, the Career Development Center celebrated its grand opening with a ribbon-cutting ceremony and open house for students. With new resources like career counseling, job shadowing opportunities, and a new resume workshop, we're excited to see how this will benefit our community in the coming years.

Overall, it has been an incredible year filled with exciting moments and new initiatives that have helped shape our campus community. We can't wait to see what next year will bring!
