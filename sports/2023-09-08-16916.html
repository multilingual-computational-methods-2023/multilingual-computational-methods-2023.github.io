1 million Euro Grant for Renewable Energy Project in Nigeria

By Sébastien Boillat
Published September 08, 2023

2019-07-15T13:46:00+01:00

Breaking News: The European Union has agreed to provide a grant of €1 million to support the implementation of the project "Renewable Energy for Improved Livelihoods in Nigeria" (REILIN) in Kebbi State, Nigeria. This project is being implemented by the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) Foundation, with funding from the European Union and technical assistance from the United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO). The grant will support the implementation of renewable energy projects such as solar electrification, wind power, and geothermal energy to improve access to electricity in rural areas. This project aims to provide clean and reliable energy to over 100,000 households in Kebbi State, reducing their reliance on fossil fuels and improving their standard of living.
