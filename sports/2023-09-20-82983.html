The United States and China have reached a trade agreement that will see the two countries reduce their trade imbalance by US$200 billion over the next two years. The deal, which was announced by President Trump on Friday, is expected to be signed in the coming weeks. This is a significant step towards resolving the ongoing trade war between the two nations.

The agreement includes commitments from both sides to reduce tariffs and increase imports of each other's goods. China will also agree to buy more US agricultural products, while the US will lower its import taxes on Chinese goods. The deal also includes provisions for intellectual property protection and enforcement.

The trade war between the United States and China has had a significant impact on both countries and the global economy as a whole. The agreement is seen as an important step towards resolving the conflict and restoring stability to international trade relations.
